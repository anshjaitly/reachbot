Phase 1 — End Effector
DESIGN ITERATIONS

Four design iterations — each rejected for a specific engineering reason — led to the ORCA Hand v1. Every pivot is documented below with the exact failure mode that drove the change.

01
REJECTED
Dual-Paddle Claw
Single MG996R drives two mirrored PLA paddles via steel linkage rods. 32–200mm span. Simple to CAD, fast to print. Failure: flat paddles slip on round objects — no surface conform. A bottle or can with no flat face has zero friction contact. No finger articulation means objects over 32mm but under ~60mm always slip free.
× REJECTED — slip on curved objects, no conform grip
02
REJECTED
Octopus Tentacles (3+3)
Flexible TPU tentacles — 3 left + 3 right — wrapping passively around objects like a real cephalopod arm. Concept: no rigid grip force, just conforming contact. Failure: zero positional stiffness. Tentacles that flex under gravity cannot reliably close to a defined span or deliver consistent grip. No rigid structure = no repeatable positioning for inverse kinematics.
× REJECTED — no rigid structure, non-repeatable grip
03
REJECTED
6-Tentacle Controlled Array
6 individually-cable-driven TPU tentacles with a central hub. Each tentacle independently actuated for more precise closure. Failure: cable routing requires 6 individual servo channels to be meaningful. Under $300 budget allows 1–2 extra servos max. Routing 6 independent tendons in a compact hub is mechanically intractable at this scale without a custom PCB.
× REJECTED — 6-channel requirement exceeds budget
04
CURRENT
ORCA Hand v1 — ETH Zurich Open Source
17-DOF anthropomorphic tendon-driven hand. All finger flexor tendons tied to a single anchor on the MG996R servo horn — full fist-close in one motion. Rigid PLA phalanges give positional accuracy; tendon routing gives conform grip. Printed in PLA at 50% infill, 90% modifier zones at joint pivots on Bambu X1 Carbon. 78.66g · 7h 33m print time. Wrist auto-leveling servo added: wrist_deg = 90 + shoulder − elbow. Tendon hardware: Power Pro 30lb braid + 2mm steel pins + 4×8×3mm bearings.
✓ ACTIVE — printed, assembled, in wrist CAD phase

Engineering Calculations
CALC 01 — ARM LENGTH
arm_length ≥ forward_reach_max + joint_offset arm_length ≥ 650mm + 110mm = 760mm
→ Final arm tube: 760mm (2.5ft)
110mm offset accounts for shoulder joint inset from armrest edge. Gripper tip reaches any object within a typical user's maximum forward reach zone.
CALC 02 — SHOULDER TILT
floor_angle = arctan(720 / 760) = 43.5° → design 52° high_angle = arctan(150 / 760) = 11° → design 28°
→ Range: −52° (floor) to +28° (high shelf)
Extra margin beyond arctan minimum so gripper reaches objects directly below the chair. High angle covers standard desk height above armrest level.
CALC 03 — SHOULDER TORQUE
τ_min = 0.35kg × 9.8 × 0.38m = 1.30 Nm = 13.3 kg·cm 2× MG996R = 2 × 9.4 = 18.8 kg·cm
→ 2× MG996R gives 44% safety margin
0.35kg estimated for aluminum + PLA + wrist servo + gripper. Safety margin handles dynamic shock from object pickup, not just static weight.
CALC 04 — GRIPPER SPAN
pen (14mm) → closed 32mm, Sugru -4mm = 28mm ✓ bottle (80mm) → open 200mm = 60mm clearance ✓
→ 32mm closed, 200mm open — covers all targets
Sugru tips compress 3–4mm under grip force, effectively narrowing closed span for thin objects. 200mm gives 60mm clearance around a standard bottle.
CALC 05 — FLOOR REACH
reach_to_floor = 760mm × sin(52°) = 599mm clearance = 720mm - 599mm = 121mm ✓
→ Gripper reaches floor with 121mm margin
Mounting at armrest height means design auto-adapts per user chair. 121mm clearance at full 52° tilt confirmed.

Build Instructions
ASSEMBLY ORDER
01
Print ORCA Hand digits ✓ Done
5 digits exported from Onshape STEP → Bambu Studio. 50% infill base, 90% modifier zones at all joint pivot regions. 0.2mm layer height, 4 walls, black PLA. Print palm and phalanges together per-digit.
→ Complete: 78.66g, 7h 33m on Bambu X1 Carbon
02
Press bearings into knuckle housings
Press 4×8×3mm MR84ZZ bearings into each high-load knuckle joint housing. Use a flat surface and thumb pressure — do not hammer. Bearing should seat flush with outer face of housing.
→ Test: bearing should spin freely with no lateral play
⚠ Crooked press-in = joint binding. Seat perpendicular.
03
Insert 2mm steel pivot pins
Push 2mm steel pins through bearing inner races and pivot holes of each phalange joint. Pins must be flush on both ends — file if protruding. Each finger has 3 joints × 1 pin = 15 pins total + thumb.
→ Each joint should flex freely with no lateral wobble
04
Route Power Pro tendon per finger
Thread Power Pro 30lb braid through the tendon channel on the flexor side of each phalange, anchoring at the fingertip. Keep tension consistent — slack = limp finger. Route all tendons through palm channel to servo horn anchor point.
→ 5 tendons total, all terminating at servo horn
⚠ Do not knot — use crimp sleeve or heat-seal end.
05
Anchor all tendons to MG996R horn
All 5 flexor tendons tie to a single anchor point on the MG996R servo horn. Pre-tension each tendon to match — unequal tension = uneven curl. Use the hole at 18–20mm from shaft center for anchor attachment.
→ Horn at 90° = hand open. Full rotation = full fist
06
Test fist-close with servo tester
Plug MG996R into tester center channel. Insert 4 AA batteries. Slowly rotate knob from open to closed. All 5 fingers should curl simultaneously into a fist. Check each finger curls symmetrically.
→ SUCCESS = full fist with no finger lagging
⚠ One finger doesn't curl → re-tension that tendon
07
CAD wrist modification (in progress)
Onshape: add tendon channel through wrist housing and MG996R mount cut for wrist auto-leveling servo. Wrist_deg = 90 + shoulder_deg − elbow_deg. This compensates for combined link bend during arm positioning.
→ In progress — tendon channel and mount cuts defined
08
Grip test + document results
Try gripping: TV remote, full water bottle, pen, phone. Log object width, grip success, and any slip. Data feeds directly into Regeneron STS research paper and YOLOv8n object-width calibration.
→ Document everything — this is your baseline research
Engineering Risk Analysis
FAILURE MODES
TENDON
One finger doesn't curl
Fix: Re-tension that tendon — slack at anchor. Pull tighter before re-anchoring to horn.
JOINT
Finger joint binds / stiff
Fix: Bearing not seated flush. Press again with flat plate. Check pin for burrs — file smooth.
GRIP
Hand can't fully close to fist
Fix: Tendon slack — re-anchor all tendons with equal tension. Check horn anchor point hasn't slipped.
TENDON
Tendon snaps under load
Fix: Power Pro 30lb is over-spec for hand loads. Check crimp/anchor point — usually fails there, not mid-line.
SERVO
MG996R buzzes, no motion
Fix: Tendons too tight — servo stalls against own torque limit. Reduce pre-tension slightly.
PRINT
Phalange joint cracks under grip
Fix: Joint infill modifier not applied. Re-slice with 90% infill zone at all pivot housings.
POWER
Servo tester unresponsive
Fix: Check AA batteries — need at least 5V. Replace if low.
WRIST
Wrist doesn't level during reach
Fix: Verify wrist_deg formula: = 90 + shoulder_deg − elbow_deg. Check PCA9685 channel mapping in config.py.
